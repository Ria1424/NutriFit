Understanding Insulin Resistance in PCOS

Over 70% of women with PCOS suffer from insulin resistance, regardless of body weight. When the cells resist insulin, the pancreas responds by producing more of it. Elevated insulin directly signals the ovaries to secrete excess androgens (male hormones), causing cycle irregularity, hair thinning, and hormonal acne.

Key Takeaway:

Prioritize complex carbohydrates with low Glycemic Index (oats, quinoa, brown rice) and always pair carbs with healthy fats (avocado) or protein (tofu, egg) to prevent rapid glucose spikes.

The Science Behind Seed Cycling

Seed cycling is a gentle, natural hormone balancing protocol that supports different phases of your menstrual cycle.

Phase 1: Follicular (Days 1 - 14)

Eat **Flax** and **Pumpkin** seeds. Flax seeds contain lignans which bind to excess estrogen. Pumpkin seeds are exceptionally high in Zinc, supporting healthy progesterone creation for the next phase.

Phase 2: Luteal (Days 15 - 28)

Eat **Sesame** and **Sunflower** seeds. Sesame seeds contain lignans that keep estrogen in check. Sunflower seeds are packed with Selenium and Vitamin E, which support corpus luteum health and progesterone production.

Cortisol Management & Workout Synchronization

Intense, chronic cardiovascular workouts can trigger sustained high levels of cortisol (stress hormone), which worsens insulin resistance. To balance your hormones, sync workouts with your menstrual cycle phases:

  • Menstrual phase: Focus on restorative stretching, gentle yoga, and light walking. Keep heart rate low to promote healing.
  • Follicular phase: Transition into progressive strength training and brisk walking. Energy is rising.
  • Ovulatory phase: High energy peak. Excellent time for high intensity interval training (HIIT) or heavy strength lifts.
  • Luteal phase: Energy begins to taper. Transition back to pilates, steady-state cardio, or slow strength flows.
